Vardal Turnforening's project

Duration

9 weeks

Date

2023

Tools

Figma, Adobe Indesign

My role

Insight work, sketch, storyboarding, graphic designelements, lo-fi/hi-fi prototyping, Visual Design

Background

Vardal Gymnastics Club is part of the Innlandet Gymnastics and Turn Circuit, offering gymnastics for children, youth, and adults. Established in 1933, Vardal Gymnastics Club has not made many changes over the years in managing communication related to their activities.

Currently, they use various digital solutions such as their website, Spond, Facebook, text messages, and email to manage their affairs. While the current approach works to some extent, it is not optimal for a good user experience. The existing solution lacks efficiency, which can be improved by consolidating all communication and information in one place.

I have been assigned the task in this project and worked on it as a member of an interdisciplinary collaboration group, along with three other team members.

About the project

Main goal

The goal was to conceptualize and design a new system and solution that ensures a modern and good user experience for our target audience, including parents of different ages, board members, and coaches in Vardal Gymnastics Club. Additionally, we aimed to update Vardal's visual identity, intending to uphold their core values in the new solution

Target audience

The target audience are designed for consists of parents of performers, coaches, and board members at Vardal. Since we have a diverse audience, it is important to consider the age range as well as varying levels of technological knowledge. In addition to current users, we also aim to appeal to potential future users.

The problem

The problem addressed in this project was the inefficiency of Vardal Gymnastics Club's current communication and information management system.

The solution

Conceptualizing and designing a new communication and information management system for Vardal Gymnastics Club. Our team aimed to create a streamlined and user-friendly platform that consolidates all communication channels into one.

Design Process

During the timeline of this project, we were following the design thinking process, which offers a structured and user-centric framework. This ensured that the solutions developed are not only visually appealing but also functionally effective and emotionally resonant with users.

User interview

We interviewed a parent of a performer, and before that, we requested a consent statement for the interview and audio recording. Doing this before conducting the interview is important for ethical and legal reasons. It ensures that the participant is fully informed about the nature of the interview, including its purpose, the potential use of audio recording, and how their information may be utilized.

We also had access to interviews conducted by other groups with coaches, board members, and other parents, which was essential for defining specific strategic goals and identifying the target audience.

Doing a user interview is an important part of understanding our users. This helps us learn about the user's experiences, as well as their requirements and needs for the solution.

User personas

We developed five personas and ten scenarios to ensure a deeper understanding of users and their various needs, requirements, and goals. We then selected two personas with one scenario for each. This allowed us to create visual representations of the target audience we were designing for and contributed to a more comprehensive understanding of their experiences.

Affinity map

We proceeded in the process by creating an affinity map to group the findings. We systematically worked through each user group, starting by analyzing the interviews conducted by both our team and the other groups. Utilizing this method enabled us to organize insights into specific categories, providing a better overview of user needs and requirements.

Need specifications

For the parents:

  • A primary theme among parents was the need for a clearer overview.

  • Parents, especially those with multiple enrolled performers, expressed feeling overwhelmed and struggled to differentiate between their children's various activities. They also desired an improved notification system, as it was easy to miss important alerts among the abundance of irrelevant ones.

Additionally, finding information was challenging due to Vardal's use of various digital platforms such as Facebook and Spond for communication and information sharing. The most significant needs include having a better overview of children's activities, improved access to payment and registration information, centralized communication, and a user-friendly online store

For the coaches:

The coaches did not express many complaints about the current solution, but they had some needs they wanted addressed. For efficient activity planning, coaches emphasized the importance of knowing the number of participants in advance, along with being aware of the availability of assistant coaches. There was also a need for a more organized, modern, and appealing system

Functional description

The main functions prioritized for parents include

  • a simple and intuitive navigation system that consolidates all information and communication in one place.

  • The homepage should be clear, uncomplicated, and ensure easy access to information, especially during the registration process.

  • For new members, we aim to include an indicator showing their position on the waiting list.

Once allocated a spot in Vardal, users should access their calendar and have the ability to sign up for relevant training sessions. Another important feature is the ability to customize notifications so that parents can control what they are alerted about.

Lastly, we have considered improving the overview of multiple children and their profiles, allowing users to switch between their children's profiles on the main menu.

Coaches will have the same layout as parents, but with functionality tailored to their needs. The idea is for coaches to be able to edit and input general information about their training sessions, making it easy to make changes on the go as needed. Additionally, coaches have an overview of the number of participants and the ability to select additional assistant coaches based on availability. Coaches should also have access to a group chat for coaches, where they can plan and discuss upcoming training sessions and events.

Site map

To help visualize and delineate the hierarchy, we created a sitemap. This is designed to illustrate the relationship between information elements and is used to display the organization and navigation of a website.

Creating a sitemap contributed to visualizing how we wanted the hierarchy on the website to be. It provided a visualization of the natural way for users to navigate through the website, giving us an understanding of the overall layout.

Concept sketches

After structuring, we began working on the 'skeleton' and generated ideas on how we wanted the interface to look and which elements should be included based on Persona 1 for parents and Persona 1 for coaches.

The focus here was on the placement of buttons, fields, and other components on the interface.

We then started with individual sketches to conceptualize how we envisioned the solution. Subsequently, we collaborated to combine elements from each sketch into a common draft, which we further developed into various wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes.

Design manual

To attract users and provide a great experience, we focused on the surface design, including layout, colors, typography, images, and icons. We maintained consistency on both the website and the app to make users feel familiar, regardless of how they navigate. Our goal was to strike a balance between aesthetics and user-friendliness, ensuring every detail met user needs. This user-centric approach helped us create a visually appealing and user-friendly graphic profile that meets our audience's expectations
